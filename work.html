Teaching


ALA 320/321

"The Program on Intergroup Relations (IGR) is a social justice education program. IGR blends theory and experiential learning to facilitate students' learning about social group identity, social inequality, and intergroup relations. The program prepares students to live and work in a diverse world and educates them in making choices that advance equity, justice, and peace." (From the IGR website.)

ALA 320 is the IGR peer-facilitator training program, and ALA 321 is the facilitator Practicum. I was a Graduate Student Instructor (GSI) for 4 semesters of ALA 321 and 1 semester of ALA 320. Librarianship


Clinton-Macomb Public Library Subject Headings Project

Part of my work as a librarian at the Clinton-Macomb Public Library includes working on our Subject Headings Update Project. Subject Headings are an essential part of cataloging, and significantly impact the findability of materials and patron catalog search experience. The report below was submitted to the Director of the Library for review with our recommendations for first-round subject heading updates.


Clinton-Macomb Public Library Picture Book Audit

As part of my capstone course for my Master's program, my partner and I worked with the Clinton-Macomb Public Library to conduct a diversity audit of their picture book collection. Our final deliverables included a presentation to library staff as well as a report detailing our audit process, results, and recommendations for collection development and patron engagement.
